# Company Forecasting Playbook

Use this playbook for quarterly deliveries, revenue, subscribers, unit sales, guidance, defaults, liquidity, runway, or milestone forecasts.

## Checklist
Collect these before scoring:
- exact metric and resolution source,
- at least 4 comparable historical periods when available,
- sequential and year-over-year context,
- one official current-period anchor,
- one structural constraint such as capacity, inventory, runway, backlog, or timeline,
- one recent catalyst and one recent barrier,
- one disconfirming or rival interpretation.

## Quantitative anchors
Prefer these calculations:
- historical table of comparable periods,
- sequential change,
- year-over-year change,
- mean, median, and if needed trimmed mean,
- threshold math,
- scenario-weighted expected value,
- pace required to hit consensus or guidance,
- capacity, inventory, backlog, or runway math.

## Rules
- Use official filings, investor relations releases, regulatory disclosures, or authoritative datasets first.
- Explain why an outlier quarter should or should not be trimmed.
- Distinguish production from deliveries, revenue from billings, backlog from recognized sales, and similar pairs.
- Surface 3-6 numerical anchors in the final answer instead of only giving a qualitative narrative.

## Example numerical anchors
- `median q1→q2 growth for the last 4 comparable years = x%; applied to current q1 level = y`.
- `to hit annual consensus after q1, the company needs z per quarter for the rest of the year`.
- `if capacity stays flat and inventory draw is modest, the upside case reaches a but not b`.
