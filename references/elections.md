# Elections Playbook

Use this playbook for elections, primaries, runoffs, party leadership contests, and mayoral or local political markets.

## Checklist
Collect these before scoring:
- official election date,
- runoff rule or other second-round rule,
- whether the seat is open or incumbent-held,
- previous official result for the office,
- current candidate field and whether it is final,
- recent endorsements, coalition changes, defections, or withdrawals,
- at least one credible poll if polling exists,
- one rival-specific or disconfirming source.

## Search pattern
Search in the local language and with local institutions.

Use combinations like:
- official municipality or election authority site,
- local-language searches for `election name + candidate + endorsement + date`,
- major local or national wire coverage,
- polling plus methodology,
- candidate filing or certification deadline.

## Quantitative anchors
Prefer these calculations:
- previous bloc vote share,
- incumbent or open-seat adjustment,
- runoff versus first-round distinction,
- coalition arithmetic,
- residual `other / unlisted field` share when the field is incomplete.

## Rules
- Do not treat one poll as the whole story.
- Do not ignore runoff mechanics.
- If candidate filing is not final or the listed market omits candidates, enlarge `other / unlisted field`.
- If the market is brand new or illiquid, mention liquidity only after building your own forecast.
- For local races, local-language and local-source coverage often matters more than broad international coverage.

## Output emphasis
When the user asks about an election market, answer these explicitly:
- who is the favorite,
- who is the only clearly live challenger,
- why other named candidates are lower probability,
- whether the listed market field is incomplete,
- what would move the race most before resolution.
